ABOUT

Origin

The concept for this game grew out of a fascination with time. How might time look if you twisted and looped it back on itself? To experiment, I wrote a simple story of a man repeating the same day three times. While writing, I stumbled upon the word "saltus", meaning "forest" in Latin. But to my surprise, I found it had a second meaning: a sudden break in time. So I had a word for forest that also denoted a temporal discontinuity, an unexpected and unpredictable chronological jump. Saltus grew from there.

Mechanics

The game is composed of 206 blocks of text, called nodes. Nodes connect to each other like links in a chain, creating the story. When you make a decision, the node chain branches, flowing out from the starting point like the roots of a tree. The resulting structure resembles a path cut through a forest, wandering, splitting, joining, flowing.

Decision Map

It's hard to grasp the structure of the game while playing in. To understand the dimensions of these node chains, I've created a map. Instead of visualizing space, however, it visualizes decisions. You begin in the center and move either up or down, following the flow of the story. Each tile represents a group of nodes. The resulting diagram is a topography of choices, a network of decisions.
